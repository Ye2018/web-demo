Legal and Regulatory Environment

Module 1: Awareness and Orientation

It can be scary starting up a new business! People worry that they may lose all of their investment money, never mind the disappointment of possible failure. A government can make it easier for new business people by lessening the risk of starting and running a business through laws (also known as Acts) passed by its elected officials.

There are many laws relevant to businesses in Canada. An important piece of legislation is the Competition Act. It is a federal law governing most business conduct in Canada. It contains both criminal and civil provisions aimed at preventing anti-competitive practices in the marketplace.

Regulations

Regulations (rules or orders made by government to carry out the purposes set out in legislation and statutes) interpret the law in detail and indicate how the law will be implemented and enforced.

Industry Canada (IC)

Industry Canada (IC) for example, writes regulations for business. This government agency works with Canadians in all areas of the economy and in all parts of the country to improve conditions for investment, enhance Canada’s innovation performance, increase Canada’s share of global trade and build a fair, efficient and competitive marketplace.
